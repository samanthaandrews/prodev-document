How I Use My Whole Brain

I use my whole brain when I am creating. Whether it be writing blog posts, writing code, or designing, I am hitting all four quadrants. Even when I am writing creatively I am using the logical and analytical side of my brain because I want the flow and pacing of the writing to make sense to the reader. Coding also feels like I am using my whole brain. I get to be creative with the design, but there are limits since I want users (other humans) to be able to use my code. This means my design has to be user-friendly and logical. In addition, writing code that others will be able to understand means I have to use the organizational quadrant of my brain. Graphic design was my introduction to using my whole brain. I found that when I was designing and working with clients, I was completely immersed in the task at hand. Losing track of time is a clue that points to whole-brain activities.
