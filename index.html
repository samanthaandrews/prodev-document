How I Use My Whole Brain

I use my whole brain when I am creating. Whether it be writing blog posts, writing code, or designing, I am hitting all four quadrants. Even when I am writing creatively I am using the logical and analytical side of my brain because I want the flow and pacing of the writing to make sense to the reader.

Coding also feels like I am using my whole brain. I get to be creative with the design, but there are limits since I want users (other humans) to be able to use my code. This means my design has to be user-friendly and logical. In addition, writing code that others will be able to understand means I have to use the organizational quadrant of my brain.

Graphic design was my introduction to using my whole brain. I found that when I was designing and working with clients, I was completely immersed in the task at hand. Losing track of time is a clue that points to whole-brain activities.

Worth Questions

  1. What are internal and external factors keeping you from achieving your goal?

    Self doubt and imposter syndrome are internal factors that weigh me down sometimes. External factors include lack of time and experience actually programming.

  2. How do personally create value for others?

    I always help with simple design projects when people ask for help. I think my writing creates value for others, and that's something I love to do. I love giving personal and professional advice to people who ask for it!

  3. Has anyone ever told you that you got potential professionally? Do you believe it?

    I do believe I have potential professionally. In previous jobs, my supervisors have all liked me, but I felt limited by their lack of planning or organization. I often felt like I was taking things more seriously than anyone else, and that my success was limited by a supervisor's lack of support or inability to take responsibility for their mistakes. This makes it sound like I am blaming everyone else for my problems, but I do hope to one day find a supervisor who works as hard as I do and who inspires me to step outside my comfort zone. I want to learn how to be a great leader from a great leader.

Weekly Reflections

Week 1

I know I'm still very firmly in the honeymoon phase of this bootcamp, but I'm loving it thus far. I realize I am interested in both frontend and backend development. I seek to understand as much as possible about whatever we are learning and I like asking other students for help when I don't understand something. Everyone has their strengths and I appreciate being part of a team. I think the immersive, fast-paced environment fits my personality and learning style.

Week 2

This week has been more difficult than Week 1, mostly just because the material is more difficult to understand. It's hard to go to bed each night with a jumble of php syntax floating around my brain! I know that if I keep practicing and asking questions during class, it will eventually start to come together. I've found that for me, the first 50 hours of learning and doing something is the most frustrating. After that, it's still not easy, but at least the pieces make sense. In my personal life I've been having to say "no" a lot to friends who ask me to hang out. Even though everyone knows I'm in the bootcamp I don't think they fully understand the intensity! A year ago I would have felt guilty for saying "no," but at this point in my life I no longer feel that way.
